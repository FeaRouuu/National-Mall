The National Mall

The National Mall is a park open to the public in downtown Washington, D.C., the capital of the United States. It is bordered by numerous museums, monuments and memorials. It stretches from the Washington Monument to the United States Capitol. The Mall is one of the best known tourist spots in the city and is often used for events due to its civic and historical significance.It includes many monuments.

The Smithsonian Institution building

It houses the administrative offices of the Smithsonian Institution and an information center. Located near the National Mall in Washington and near the National Museum of African Art and the Arthur M. Sackler Gallery, the building is constructed with red sandstone in a neo-Gothic style It has been a National Historic Landmark since 1965 and has been listed on the National Register of Historic Places since 1966. The National Gallery of Art

The National Gallery of Art is an American museum in Washington, D.C., one of the largest in the world, owned by the United States government, and admission is free. The collections are distributed in two buildings linked by an underground gallery under the Mall: the East building and the West building. The National Gallery of Art is affiliated with the Smithsonian Institution.

The Washington Monument

The Washington Monument is an obelisk-shaped tower over 169 meters high. It was in February 1885 in honor of George Washington that it was inaugurated. The construction work lasted almost forty years, from 1848 to 1885. At its completion, until the erection of the Eiffel Tower in 1889, it was the tallest monument in the world with 169 m. In September 1833, the Washington National Monument Society was created, by John Marshall and James Madison, to raise funds to build a monument in memory of George Washington. In 1836, she launched a competition to decide the nature of the monument, won by Robert Mills. In 1847, she believed that she had collected enough money to begin construction of the monument. The Washington Monument had to be built at the crossroads between a line going south from the White House and a line going west of the Capitol (on the site of what is now Jefferson Pier). However, the soil there could not have supported the structure. It was therefore erected at the western end of the National Mall.
